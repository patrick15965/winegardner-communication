OVERVIEW
--------
Patrick participated in a process improvement interview at Wine Gardener with two consultants (one named Jordan) who are gathering information about operational inefficiencies, training gaps, and communication breakdowns between field operations and back-office accounting. Patrick shared insights from his unique perspective having transitioned from the project/field side to the billing/accounting side approximately since late April/early June.


ATMOSPHERE
----------
Professional but candid and somewhat frustrated — Patrick was open about systemic problems while being careful not to overstep given he's still learning his new role. The interviewers were probing and analytical, trying to surface specific process breakdowns and potential solutions.


KEY TAKEAWAYS
-------------
  - Training deficiencies are a major theme: Patrick had to learn critical skills like reading plans and handling submittals largely on his own, eventually taking an external class for plan reading; he has raised this issue before
  - Contract extraction process is error-prone: Patrick manually pulls key information (labor compliance, scope of work, bond requirements, OSIP/SIF status) from executed contracts into shared drive folders, but receives conflicting guidance and gets discouraged from contacting general contractors for clarification
  - Communication gap between ops and accounting is significant: Patrick now speaks with Jeffy multiple times daily in his current role, but when in ops had difficulty getting responses from Tucker and would often have to physically go find him for urgent matters
  - Tools mentioned: Field Flow, Stack, and Foundation are the primary software systems; contract-related work happens outside these tools in shared drives
  - Patrick's current responsibilities: Invoice processing, weekly GL downloads/editing/exports to a third party for Foundation entry, budget imports into Foundation, pre-qualifications for estimating
  - Organizational tension around accountability: People "dip their hands into things" creating overlap; former employees who created problems are no longer around to be held accountable; there's a culture of "trust without verification" that creates tension
  - Patrick's background: About 9 months in ops at Wine Gardener, previously worked in Amazon space and briefly at an optometry practice; came from non-construction background
